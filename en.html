About SIA

What is SIA?

SIA (Security iD Automatic) is a next-generation biometric identity system designed to completely replace passwords, ID cards, and all traditional forms of authentication.

Instead of something you know (a password) or something you have (a card), SIA authenticates by what you are — your biological self. Cannot be copied, cannot be stolen, cannot be forgotten.

Designed to operate fully automatically across every area of life — from banking transactions to border control, from medical visits to personal device login.

How It Works

Simple for the user.
Impossible for attackers.

01

Biometric Data Collection

The system simultaneously collects data from all 5 biometric factors through integrated sensor devices — cameras, microphones, fingerprint sensors, and iris scanners. The process is natural, requiring no complex interaction from the user.

⏱️ < 0.1 seconds
02

AI Encryption & Processing

Data is instantly encrypted and fed into a multi-layer AI analysis system. Each factor is processed in parallel by specialized deep learning models, generating a unique "biometric signature" for this authentication session.

🔐 End-to-end Encrypted
03

Matching & Verification

The biometric signature is compared against a securely stored profile in a distributed database. An algorithm combines results from all 5 factors to deliver an authentication decision with extremely high accuracy, eliminating misidentification risk.

🧠 Multi-factor AI Fusion
04

Confirmation & Access Grant

Within under one second, the system sends an identity confirmation signal to the requesting service. The user is instantly granted access — no additional input needed, no waiting, no extra steps.

✅ Total time < 1 second
05

Logging & Continuous Security

Every authentication session is logged with timestamps and security metadata. The system monitors for unusual patterns and automatically triggers alerts when suspicious activity is detected, protecting users 24/7.

📋 Full Audit Trail

Frequently Asked Questions

Want to know more?

Is my biometric data secure?
Absolutely. SIA never stores raw biometric data. Instead, the system converts your data into a one-way encrypted "digital signature" — it cannot be reversed to recreate your face image, fingerprint, or voice. Even if the system is attacked, bad actors cannot recover your original data.
What happens if I change my appearance or get injured?
SIA uses all 5 factors simultaneously with a flexible tolerance mechanism. If one factor temporarily fails to match (e.g., a hoarse voice from a cold, or a lightly scratched fingerprint), the remaining factors still ensure accurate authentication. For major changes (cosmetic surgery, loss of a finger), users have a controlled profile update process.
Can SIA integrate into an enterprise's existing system?
Yes. SIA provides standard RESTful APIs, multi-platform SDKs (iOS, Android, Web, Desktop), and supports popular protocols like OAuth 2.0, SAML, and OpenID Connect. Typical integration time is 3–14 business days depending on scale. The SIA technical team supports the entire deployment process.
Does SIA comply with data protection regulations?
Yes. SIA is designed to comply with Vietnam's Decree 13/2023/ND-CP on personal data protection, GDPR (Europe), and international standards ISO/IEC 30107 (biometric anti-spoofing) and ISO/IEC 19794 (biometric data). All data processing has complete audit logs.
What devices are needed to use SIA?
For individual users, modern smartphones (from 2020 onwards) already have sufficient front cameras, microphones, and fingerprint sensors for most SIA features. For enterprises deploying all 5 factors (including iris scanning and 3D bone structure), specialized sensor devices need to be integrated at access points.
Can SIA be used internationally?
Yes. SIA is designed for global application, supporting multiple languages and integrating with international identity systems. When SIA is licensed to overseas partners, registered users can use their SIA identity across borders — similar to a biometric electronic passport but far more advanced.
