Introduction

What is SIA?

Frequently Asked Questions

Want to find out more?

Is my biometric data fully protected?
Absolutely. SIA never backs up or stores raw biological imagery or voice tracking files. Instead, your entries are instantaneously translated into an encrypted, one-way cryptographic signature. Even if our system environment suffers a perimeter breach, intruders find zero readable data to harvest or reconstruct.
What happens if I alter my physical appearance or suffer an injury?
SIA monitors 5 biometric dimensions concurrently and applies robust tolerance thresholds. If a single factor experiences temporary drift (e.g., vocal hoarseness due to cold symptoms or a small finger laceration), the remaining active nodes verify your identity with precision. For major long-term shifts, secure identity governance resets are provided.
Can SIA hook directly into our active corporate architecture?
Yes. SIA provisions robust native RESTful APIs alongside cross-platform SDKs (supporting iOS, Android, Web, and Desktop targets), anchoring to traditional token handlers like OAuth 2.0, SAML, and OpenID Connect. Standard engineering onboarding cycles range between 3–14 business days depending on scale parameters.
Does the platform align with privacy data rules?
Completely. SIA is engineered explicitly around Vietnamese Decree 13/2023/NĐ-CP guidelines regarding personal data protection, European GDPR structures, and global standards including ISO/IEC 30107 (biometric presentation attack detection) and ISO/IEC 19794 framework compliance. Fully verifiable cryptographic tracking logs follow every operation.
What hardware components are necessary to deploy SIA?
For individual endpoints, modern smartphones (manufactured from 2020 onward) house adequate front cameras, microphone diaphragms, and capacitive fingerprint assemblies to trigger basic SIA layers. Enterprises looking to employ 100% factor protection can source specialized hardware terminal units for checkpoint environments.
Can I utilize my SIA credentials overseas?
Yes. SIA is built for international scalability, hosting multi-lingual string matrices and linking to global identity protocols. When foreign node operators authorize the SIA framework within their regions, your pre-existing profile securely matches credentials across boundaries — functioning much like an advanced digital biometric passport system.
